= Composter

The Composter turns *organic material* — crops, leaves, wood, biomass, even alien remains — into *Fertilizer*, the fluid that powers the high-yield *fertilised* crop recipes. It is the supply side of the fertilised farming loop: compost surplus crops into Fertilizer, pipe that Fertilizer back into your xref:/ff/buildings/FarmingPlots.adoc[plots] through an xref:/ff/buildings/FarmingModules.adoc[Irrigation Module], and harvest several times more per cycle.

[NOTE]
====
*In-game description* +
"Used to create fertilizer from organic products such as crops and leaves into fertilizer for a higher crop output"
====

[NOTE]
====
*Fertilizer and Water are fluids* whose recipe amounts are given in litres, but represented in the game user interface as *m³*. `3000` liters is *3{nbsp}m³*.
====

== How it works

* Feed the Composter an organic input plus *Water*; it runs a *compost recipe* and outputs *Fertilizer*.
* Most compost recipes share the same base numbers, so unless a recipe is different, each cycle takes *6 seconds* and yields *3000{nbsp}L (3{nbsp}m³) of Fertilizer*. Higher-value inputs (biomass, biofuel, alien remains) raise the output up to 4000–7500{nbsp}L.
* The Fertilizer output is a fluid, it must be moved by pipe into your plots.

== Milestones

[%header,cols="1,2"]
|===
| | Composter

| Available with
| HUB Tier 5 — "Fertiliser Buildings" milestone
|===

The Composter unlocks at *Tier 5*, on the "Fertiliser Buildings" milestone (which also grants the compost recipes and a couple of alternate biomass recipes). Note this is *before* the fertilised crop recipes themselves, which unlock at Tier 6 — so you can build up a Fertilizer supply first, then switch plots to fertilised recipes.

== Tips

* *Compost your surplus.* Crops you are not eating or extracting seeds from are ideal Composter feedstock — 2 of almost any crop makes 3{nbsp}m³ of Fertilizer.
* *Leaves and wood work too.* Cheap biomass sources (leaves, wood) compost at the same 3{nbsp}m³/6{nbsp}s rate, so a nearby forest can feed the fertilised loop.
* *Match Fertilizer supply to plots.* Each fertilised plot draws 15{nbsp}m³/min of Fertilizer, so one crop-compost line (30{nbsp}m³/min) supplies roughly two fertilised plots.
