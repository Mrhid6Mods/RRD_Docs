= Seed Extractor

The Seed Extractor is the building that keeps a farm supplied with *seeds*. Rather than converting a harvested crop directly back into its own seed, it processes *dirt products* and outputs seeds. Despite the in-game description, the seed type that comes out is *not random*, but is based on the type of dirt provided. It is one of the first three farm buildings, unlocked with the very first farming milestone at Tier 2.

[NOTE]
====
*In-game description* +
"Insert dirt product to get turned into seeds. Ficsit R&D can not predict what seed will come out. and it seams seeds have a forum of rarity to the output."

(Spelling reproduced from the game files.)
====

== How it works

* The extractor takes a *dirt product* as its input. Ficsit Farming has three dirt items, any of which is a valid "dirt product":
+
[%header,cols="2,3"]
|===
| Item | Made from

| Jar of Dirt
| Mined from a Dirt resource node, or `Limestone -> Jar of Dirt`

| Jar of Peat Soil (fertilised dirt)
| `Jar of Dirt + Fertilizer`

| Jar of Bog Earth (wet dirt)
| `Jar of Dirt + Water`
|===
* While the building is *powered* and has dirt in its input, it runs and outputs *seeds* to its output inventory / belt. The output seed variety is based on dirt tpe, so a single extractor gradually supplies a mix of seed types rather than one chosen seed.
* The extractor will only run when *all* of the following are true:
** it is powered;
** there is a dirt product in its input;
** there is enough of that dirt product to consume; and
** there is room in the output for the produced seeds.

[NOTE]
====
*Because the output contains multiple seed types, plan for a buffer/sorter on the output if you need a specific seed type.*
====

== Tips

* *Feed it dirt, not crops.* The extractor's input is a dirt product; keep a supply of Jar of Dirt (from a Dirt node or from Limestone) flowing into it.
* *Expect a seed mix.* Route the seeds through storage or a smart splitter if a plot needs one specific seed type.
* *It is a Tier 2 building* and comes online with your first plot, so set up a dirt supply early to keep seeds flowing into the xref:/ff/buildings/FarmingPlots.adoc[plots].
