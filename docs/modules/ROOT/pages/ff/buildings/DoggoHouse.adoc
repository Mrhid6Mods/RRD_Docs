= Doggo House

The Doggo House is Ficsit Farming's late-game novelty building: it *houses Lizard Doggos*, keeps them fed, watered, and warm, and, per its in-game description, *extracts resources from them*. It is unlocked at Tier 6 on the "Rancher" milestone and consumes xref:/ff/buildings/CookingHub.adoc[Doggo Chow] and Water.

[NOTE]
====
*In-game description* +
"Used to house up to 16 doggos to be fed, watered, pet and heaten up while also extracting resources from them"
====

== How it works

* The house holds *up to 16 doggos*. You add a doggo by feeding it a *Full Doggo Cage* on one of its conveyor inputs; the house then keeps that doggo housed.
* Each doggo must be *fed and watered*:
** *Food:* Doggo Chow, delivered on a conveyor.
** *Water:* piped in.
* Food and water *consumption scale with the number of doggos* — the building recalculates its draw whenever the doggo count changes.
* It only produces when it has doggos, chow, and water available and there is space for the output.

[WARNING]
====
*The exact output ("resources extracted from the doggos") is not predetermined.* The in-game description says the house extracts resources, and the building has an output conveyor, but there is no fixed recipe that spells out a clear output *item* or a *per-doggo output rate*, rather this is based on normal doggo random drops and drop rate. The Doggo Chow and Water consumption *amounts per cycle* are similarly calculated on the fly from the doggo count rather than being fixed values, so they are not stated here. These are *flagged as unverified* rather than guessed.
====

The Tier 6 "Rancher" milestone also unlocks the *Doggo Chow* recipe (cooked in the xref:/ff/buildings/CookingHub.adoc[Cooking Hub]) and the Doggo Cage recipe used to catch/house doggos.

== Tips

* *Line up a Doggo Chow supply first.* The house needs a steady feed of xref:/ff/buildings/CookingHub.adoc[Doggo Chow] (35 Yosse Berry + 35 Mycelia + 25{nbsp}m³ Water per batch) plus piped Water before it will keep doggos happy.
* *More doggos means more consumption.* Food and water draw scale with the number of doggos housed, so grow the population in step with your chow/water throughput.
