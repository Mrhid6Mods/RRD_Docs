= Water Dam

The Water Dam is a large structure that spans a waterfall to generate power. Where the xref:/rp/buildings/waterturbines/index.adoc[Water Turbine] is a single machine on a small node, the Water Dam is a big buildable that stretches across a gap and produces *Power* and optionally *Water* for free once completed.

[NOTE]
====
*In-game description* +
"A Mighty Water Dam spanning waterfalls."
====

== How it works

* Water Dams are placed on a dedicated *Water Dam Node*, a special node found with the *resource scanner*. The node marks a valid waterway where a dam can be built.
* Building the dam places a series of *sections* (each *10{nbsp}m* wide) that span the waterway. The dam auto-builds its sections to bridge the gap.
* Once complete, the dam generates power and/or water continuously.
* Maximum production is set by the number of sections, not counting end peices, of the Water Dam. Each section adds 50MW and 60m³/min of water to the dam's maximum output.

[NOTE]
====
Dam nodes are often, but not always, found at the top of waterfalls. +
This includes some of the waterfalls along the edges of the map!
====

== Building Information

[%header,cols="1,1"]
|===
| | Water Dam

| Build Cost
| 5x Computer +
5x Heavy Modular Frame +
10x Rubber +
50x Concrete

| Placement
| On a *Water Dam Node* (found with the scanner); auto-builds 10{nbsp}m sections across the waterway

| Power Output
| 25 to 700 MW 

|Water Output (optional)
| 60 m³/min to 840 m³/min
|===

[NOTE]
====
Water output from the dams is is optional. If turned on, outputing water costs half of the dam's power output.
====

500x Quickwire +

The same Tier 5 milestone also unlocks the xref:/rp/buildings/windturbines/index.adoc[Wind Turbine Mk2], and adds *Water Dam Nodes to your resource scanner* so you can locate valid dam sites like ore nodes.



== Tips

* You need a *Water Dam Node* to build one. Scan for them the same way you scan for the smaller xref:/rp/buildings/waterturbines/index.adoc[Water Turbine] nodes.
* Like Water Turbines, dam power is *steady* (no weather dependence), so it suits baseload duty alongside intermittent xref:/rp/buildings/windturbines/index.adoc[wind] and xref:/rp/buildings/solarpanels/index.adoc[solar] power.
